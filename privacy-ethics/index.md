    <h1>Privacy &amp; Ethics</h1>
    <p>Some work stays private because it contains student, participant, organizational, or infrastructure details that should not become portfolio material. That boundary is part of the practice, not an omission to hide.</p>

    <h2>How to read this site</h2>
    <ul class="atlas-todo">
      <li>The portfolio carries public claims and selected proof objects.</li>
      <li>Atlas carries relationships between projects, tools, scenes, learning systems, and infrastructure.</li>
      <li>Method notes carry reusable patterns such as evidence, documentation, assumptions, and consent-forward design.</li>
      <li>Source repositories carry the live implementation truth for each public project.</li>
      <li>Evidence status is part of the work. A page may be a prototype, study, public-safe excerpt, or needs-proof stub on purpose.</li>
    </ul>

    <h2>What is intentionally withheld</h2>
    <ul class="atlas-todo">
      <li>Student or participant identities, images, and classroom data unless explicitly public-safe.</li>
      <li>Private network, home, studio, or security topology.</li>
      <li>Unredacted operations notes, credentials, or deployment details.</li>
      <li>Claims about adoption, outcomes, or reliability that do not yet have public evidence.</li>
    </ul>
