    <h1>Consent-Forward Systems</h1>
    <p>Consent, transparency, and user autonomy are treated as system behavior, not only as policy text. The question is not just what a project can sense or retain, but how those choices are made legible and reversible for the people in the room.</p>

    <h2>Operational defaults</h2>
    <ul class="atlas-todo">
      <li>Prefer local-first processing and short-lived data.</li>
      <li>Make capture state, deletion paths, and refusal visible.</li>
      <li>Avoid public proof that depends on identifiable participants when staged or redacted alternatives can do the work.</li>
      <li>Use diagrams, workshop materials, and source documentation when direct participant imagery would overreach.</li>
    </ul>

    <h2>Projects that carry the method</h2>
    <ul class="atlas-todo">
      <li><a href="/atlas/n/humanbuffer/">Human-Buffer</a> is the clearest public example.</li>
      <li><a href="/atlas/n/memory-engine/">Memory Engine</a> extends the question into retention, decay, retrieval, and revocation.</li>
      <li><a href="/atlas/n/classhub/">Class Hub</a> applies the same ethic to teaching infrastructure and controlled data handling.</li>
    </ul>
